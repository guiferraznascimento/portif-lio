Industrial Designer Student

Gui is a Product Designer based in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, working at the intersection of authorial product design, digital fabrication and bio-inspired design (biomimicry). His work explores how emerging technologies, such as 3D printing, can reshape the way products are conceived, produced and experienced, with a strong focus on footwear design and innovative wearable systems.

His practice is driven by experimentation and research, especially in the application of biomimetic principles to develop functional mechanisms, structures and forms inspired by nature. He is particularly interested in tactile movement, adaptive systems and how design can create new interactions between body, material and function. His work seeks to balance aesthetics, performance and manufacturability, translating conceptual ideas into viable and producible solutions.

Gui is currently pursuing a Bachelors degree in Design, while also developing independent projects and entrepreneurial initiatives in the field of 3D-printed products. He is the founder of a design-driven venture focused on additive manufacturing, where he explores new business models for customizable and small-scale production.

His recent projects include the development of 3D-printed footwear with integrated tightening mechanisms inspired by biological systems, as well as medical-oriented solutions such as syringe support devices for facial harmonization procedures. His experience combines product development, prototyping, cost structuring and digital fabrication processes, with an emphasis on innovation, usability and market viability.

Gui is particularly interested in the future of decentralized production, sustainable design strategies and the role of biomimicry in creating meaningful, efficient and adaptable products. Through his work, he aims to contribute to a new design paradigm that integrates technology, nature and human-centered thinking.
