LLANA

2024

Biomimetic bag inspired by armadillo.

Llana is a product design project developed as a team, combining ethnographic research, biomimicry, and textile manufacturing to create a functional, versatile bag with its own visual identity.

The starting point was nature. The team adopted a biomimic approach as a creative guiding thread, seeking formal and conceptual inspiration for the bag in the annatto  a native South American fruit whose red seeds are clustered inside a hollow, spiny fruit.

The morphology of the fruit, which opens and closes to reveal its contents, directly guided the opening system and the organic shape of the bag.

User research was central. The team mapped the complete journey of a person who works, studies, and has an active social life  from the moment they prepare their bag in the morning to washing it on the weekend.

This map revealed real pain points: the need to have valuable items accessible and safe on the go, the bag's adaptability to different contexts throughout the day (work, college, leisure), and the importance of easy cleaning without damaging the product.

The design requirements emerged from this listening process. Standardization of attachable internal compartments, balanced weight distribution, context-adaptable straps, dynamic access locks, and wash resistance were the criteria that guided the entire development.

The creative process was iterative and documented. The sketches show the transition from the shape of the annatto fruit to the silhouette of the bag, going through studies of fit, strap adjustment, and exploration of colors and materials.

The prototypes were progressively builtfirst in paper and cardboard to validate shape and ergonomics on the body, then in acrylic batting to test volume, and finally in the final version in green denim and red tricoline, with lining and strap in grosgrain ribbon.

The result is a finished product, photographed and with its own brand. The "Llana" visual identity was developed with an organic logo and a color palette reminiscent of annatto seeds  reds, earth tones, and greens.

The final bag was photographed in a real-world context, demonstrating ergonomics and aesthetics in combination.
